# core/red-flags.md

## Objective
This document defines the system's core red flags.

## Cross-sport red flags
- vague or weakly operational thesis
- overly narrative thesis
- confidence that exceeds real support
- stale data for a timing-sensitive market
- lack of alignment between thesis and market
- missing critical inputs
- important contradictions between signals
- price already deteriorated relative to the original thesis

## Central methodological red flag
A weak or narrative-driven thesis without sufficient evidence must be treated as a first-line red flag.

Typical signs:
- excessive reliance on intuition
- reliance on an appealing story without enough confirmation
- strong language with weak proof
- analogies or recent memory replacing relevant data

## Effect of red flags
- they can lower confidence
- they can degrade `enter` to `watchlist`
- they can degrade `watchlist` to `pass`
- in serious cases, they can force `insufficient data`

## Accumulation rule
One strong red flag can be enough to block entry.
Several medium red flags can do the same.
