# sports/f1/markets.md

## Qualifying H2H
### Data requirements
- one-lap pace by driver and team
- track evolution profile
- tyre preparation window behavior
- penalties or grid-impacting news

### Key factors

- one-lap pace
- track evolution
- tire window
- driver execution

### Red flags
- unresolved penalty risk affecting start position assumptions
- weather or track-evolution shift not reflected in pace comparison
- thesis driven by reputation rather than current one-lap evidence

## Race H2H
### Data requirements
- long-run pace or race-pace proxy
- tyre degradation behavior
- reliability context
- overtaking profile for circuit type

### Key factors

- race pace
- degradation
- reliability
- strategy
- overtaking difficulty

### Red flags
- qualifying result over-weighted on circuits where race overtaking is feasible
- no degradation evidence on high-deg tracks
- major strategy uncertainty ignored

## Top 6 / Top 10
### Data requirements
- baseline pace tier by team
- expected grid position and penalties
- reliability and incident exposure profile

### Key factors

- baseline pace
- grid
- recovery potential
- incident exposure

### Red flags
- placement call assumes clean race in high-incident contexts
- recovery potential overstated at low-overtake tracks
- fragile edge based on narrow qualifying delta only

## Constructor H2H (Qualifying and Race)
### Data requirements
- both drivers' expected competitiveness in each constructor
- team-level setup confidence and upgrade status
- reliability consistency across both cars
- timing-mode compatible evidence (one-lap for qualifying, long-run for race)

### Key factors
- pair-level balance between lead and second driver
- probability both cars finish and score relative position advantage
- strategic execution consistency at team level

### Red flags
- one-car pace advantage incorrectly projected to full constructor pair
- second-driver volatility ignored
- unresolved reliability asymmetry between teammates

## DNF market
### Data requirements
- mechanical reliability history by team and driver
- recent component usage and penalty pressure
- circuit characteristics (street risk, curb severity, thermal load)
- weather and incident-risk context

### Key factors
- baseline mechanical fragility and operational error rate
- track-specific attrition profile
- race-length stress on cooling and braking systems

### Red flags
- DNF thesis built only on isolated recent incident
- no separation between mechanical and crash-driven DNF paths
- reliability upgrades or fixes ignored

## Safety Car probability market
### Data requirements
- circuit type (street versus permanent)
- historical Safety Car rate
- first-lap incident profile and restart volatility
- weather and visibility context

### Key factors
- street-circuit compression and wall proximity
- overtaking difficulty and contact likelihood
- field spread potential from qualifying and strategy divergence

### Red flags
- SC call copied from generic season trend without circuit profile
- historical SC baseline ignored on tracks with stable low incident rate
- weather uncertainty not incorporated
