# sports/football/markets.md

## Objective
Map football theses to exact football markets using market-specific data requirements, key factors, and red-flag checks.

## 1X2 / DNB / Asian Handicap
### Specific data requirements
- starting lineups or high-confidence expected lineups
- team-strength profile and recent form window
- injury/suspension context by role impact
- schedule and rest differential

### Key factors
- real superiority between teams
- draw probability and game-state incentives
- tactical fit and transition profile
- home-advantage context

### Red flags
- superiority claim based only on narrative momentum
- major lineup uncertainty in key structural roles
- price drift against thesis without new confirmatory data

## Alternative Asian Handicaps (for example -0.25, -0.75, +1.25)
### Specific data requirements
- expected goal-margin distribution or equivalent strength-gap proxy
- draw-frequency baseline for both teams
- market ladder comparison across nearby handicap lines

### Key factors
- asymmetric upside/downside of half-win and half-loss structures
- expected game-state control versus late volatility risk
- sensitivity of value to quarter-line movement

### Red flags
- no margin model but aggressive quarter-line recommendation
- thesis valid only on one exact price point with no buffer
- high variance context treated as stable margin environment

## Totals
### Specific data requirements
- chance-volume and chance-quality indicators
- pace and field-territory profile
- weather and pitch context
- referee/context factors that can alter tempo

### Key factors
- expected chance volume
- conversion and finishing profile
- state-dependent incentives (must-win, protect-lead, aggregate scenarios)

### Red flags
- total thesis based only on final-score recency
- weather or lineup conditions ignored
- no clear plan for state-dependent pace changes

## BTTS
### Specific data requirements
- offensive creation floor for both sides
- concession tendency split by context
- lineup offensive/defensive balance

### Key factors
- ability of each side to create at least one quality scoring phase
- defensive structural vulnerabilities
- tactical matchup openness

### Red flags
- one side has unstable attacking floor but BTTS assumed safe
- heavy dependence on low-sample finishing overperformance
- mismatch between tactical profile and expected two-way scoring

## Both Teams To Score - First Half (BTTS HT)
### Specific data requirements
- first-half chance creation and concession splits
- first-half tactical intensity profile
- early-goal frequency baseline for both teams
- likely pressing and transition setup from projected lineups

### Key factors
- probability both sides create early high-quality chances
- first-half tempo sustainability
- set-piece threat concentration in opening phases

### Red flags
- first-half market taken from full-time BTTS logic without split validation
- one side routinely starts slowly but ignored in thesis
- low-liquidity BTTS HT price accepted without cross-check

## Corners markets (total corners, Asian corners handicap)
### Specific data requirements
- corners for/against splits in recent coherent window
- crossing volume and wing-progression profile
- game-state dependence of corner generation
- referee and style context affecting stoppages/tempo

### Key factors
- flank usage intensity and shot-block profile
- pressure phases likely to produce corner clusters
- relationship between expected lead/trailing states and corner volume

### Red flags
- corners thesis copied from goal thesis without style evidence
- set-piece specialist absences ignored
- corners line taken despite unstable or inconsistent corner baselines

## Cards markets (total cards, first card)
### Specific data requirements
- referee card baseline and foul profile
- team discipline trends and tactical foul frequency
- rivalry/pressure context and match stakes
- expected matchup frictions by zone and role

### Key factors
- contact intensity and transition-stop behavior
- expected emotional pressure and protest risk
- asymmetry in tactical fouling incentives

### Red flags
- no referee profile used for cards recommendation
- first-card market selected without early-duel context
- disciplinary suspensions or lineup changes ignored

## Rule
The selected market must be coherent with the thesis. A good general match read does not automatically fit every market.
